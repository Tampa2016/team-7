Approximately 1,275,000 people in the United States have sustained traumatic spinal cord injuries

Males account for 61 percent of all SCI’s and females 39 percent

Spinal cord injuries are most commonly caused by motor vehicle accidents

Sports-related spinal cord injuries occur more commonly in children and teenagers

Most spinal cord injury patients are in their teens or twenties

Currently, there is no cure for spinal cord injuries

Nerve cells of the brain and spinal cord respond to trauma and damage differently than most other cells of the body
